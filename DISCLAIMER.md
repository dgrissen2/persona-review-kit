# Disclaimer

## Real-named personas are caricature analytical lenses

This kit includes a small number of personas that share a name with real public
figures — notably **Charlie McElligott** and **Richard Feynman**.

These personas are **fictional caricatures used as analytical lenses**. They are:

- **Not** the real individuals, and **not** written by them.
- **Not** affiliated with, endorsed by, reviewed by, or associated with those individuals
  in any way.
- **Not** a representation of anything those individuals have actually said, written, or
  would say.

Each such persona is a stylized prompt that biases a language model toward a particular
*mode of analysis* (e.g. "positioning and market-structure thinking," or "explain this
simply and from first principles"). The name is shorthand for a way of looking at a
problem, nothing more.

If you are one of these individuals (or represent them) and would prefer your name not be
used, please open an issue and the persona will be renamed to a generic descriptor.

## No professional advice

Several personas use the vocabulary of finance, trading, risk, and markets. Nothing this
kit produces is financial, investment, legal, or professional advice. The personas are
prompt-engineering tools for reviewing *your own* documents and code; any market-related
language is part of the analytical lens, not a recommendation.

## Output is model-generated

All review output is generated by a language model (Claude, and optionally Codex). It can
be wrong, incomplete, or confidently mistaken. Treat every finding as a prompt for your
own judgment, not as ground truth.
